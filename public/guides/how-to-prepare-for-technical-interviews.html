How to Prepare for Technical Interviews in 2025

The complete strategy guide for coding interviews, system design, and behavioral questions

By AgentForge Career Team • January 15, 2025 • 15 min read

Key Statistic: 65% of candidates fail technical interviews due to lack of structured preparation, not coding ability. This guide provides the structure you need.

What to Expect in Technical Interviews

Technical interviews typically follow a consistent structure across tech companies:

Interview Type Duration Who It's For Focus Areas
Coding/Algorithm 45-60 min All levels Data structures, algorithms, problem-solving
System Design 45-60 min Senior+ (E4/L4 and above) Architecture, scalability, trade-offs
Behavioral 30-45 min All levels Culture fit, leadership, collaboration
Domain-Specific 30-60 min Specialized roles ML, frontend, security, etc.

The 5-Step Technical Interview Preparation Process

1

Understand the Interview Format

Different companies have different interview processes. Research your target companies:

  • Google: 4-5 rounds (2 coding, 1 system design, 1 behavioral, 1 domain)
  • Meta: 3-4 rounds (2 coding, 1 system design, 1 behavioral)
  • Amazon: 4-5 rounds (2 coding, 1 system design, 1-2 behavioral with LP questions)
  • Startups: Often 2-3 rounds (1 coding, 1 practical/take-home, 1 behavioral)

Action: Search "[Company] interview process 2025" on Glassdoor, Blind, and company engineering blogs.

2

Master Data Structures and Algorithms

Focus on these core topics in order of importance:

Priority 1 (80% of questions):

  • Arrays and strings (two pointers, sliding window)
  • Hash maps/sets (frequency counting, lookups)
  • Trees (DFS, BFS, binary search tree operations)
  • Graphs (BFS, DFS, Dijkstra, Union-Find)

Priority 2 (15% of questions):

  • Dynamic programming (memoization, tabulation)
  • Heaps/priority queues
  • Backtracking
  • Bit manipulation

Priority 3 (5% of questions):

  • Tries
  • Segment trees
  • Advanced graph algorithms
Study Strategy: Don't memorize solutions. Learn 10-15 patterns (sliding window, fast/slow pointers, merge intervals, etc.) and recognize which pattern applies to new problems.
3

Practice System Design

For senior roles (E4/L4+ at FAANG, Staff+ at startups), system design is critical:

Key Concepts to Master:

  • Scalability fundamentals: Horizontal vs vertical scaling, load balancing, caching
  • Database design: SQL vs NoSQL, indexing, sharding, replication
  • Microservices: Service boundaries, inter-service communication, service discovery
  • CAP theorem: Trade-offs between consistency and availability
  • Common patterns: CQRS, event sourcing, saga pattern

Practice Problems:

  1. Design a URL shortener (TinyURL)
  2. Design a social media news feed
  3. Design a chat application (WhatsApp)
  4. Design a ride-sharing service (Uber)
  5. Design a video streaming service (YouTube)
4

Prepare Behavioral Questions

Use the STAR method for all behavioral answers:

  • Situation: Brief context (1-2 sentences)
  • Task: Your specific responsibility
  • Action: What YOU did (focus on your contributions)
  • Result: Quantified outcome, lessons learned

Prepare 8-10 Stories Covering:

  • Leadership and influence
  • Conflict resolution
  • Failure and growth
  • Collaboration and teamwork
  • Handling ambiguity
  • Customer focus
  • Delivering results under pressure
  • Making data-driven decisions
Amazon Leadership Principles: If interviewing at Amazon, prepare 2 stories per LP (16 total). Use their STAR+ format (add "What would you do differently?" at the end).
5

Conduct Mock Interviews

Practice under realistic conditions:

Mock Interview Options:

Method Cost Pros Cons
Peer practice (Pramp, interviewing.io) Free Free, realistic Quality varies
AI mock interviews Free-$30/mo Available 24/7, instant feedback Less human nuance
Professional coaches $100-300/session Expert feedback, tailored Expensive
Company-specific prep $200-500 Insider knowledge Pricey, variable quality

Recommended: Do 5-10 mock interviews before your first real one. Start with easier problems and progress to company-specific questions.

Key Takeaways

  1. Structure beats raw talent: 65% of failures are due to poor preparation structure, not ability.
  2. Practice patterns, not problems: Learn 10-15 solution patterns that apply to hundreds of problems.
  3. Mock interviews are essential: 5-10 practice sessions significantly improve performance.
  4. System design matters for seniors: It's often the differentiator for senior-level offers.
  5. Behavioral questions decide culture fit: Don't neglect them—many candidates fail here.
