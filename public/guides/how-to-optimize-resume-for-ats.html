How to Optimize Your Resume for ATS in 2025

The complete guide to beating Applicant Tracking Systems and getting your resume into human hands

By AgentForge Career Team • January 15, 2025 • 12 min read

Key Statistic: 75% of resumes are rejected by ATS before a human ever sees them. This guide will help you be in the 25% that make it through.

What is an ATS and Why Does It Matter?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software that employers use to collect, scan, and rank job applications. When you submit your resume online, it doesn't go directly to a hiring manager—it first passes through an ATS that:

  • Parses your resume into structured data (name, contact info, work history, skills)
  • Scans for keywords matching the job description
  • Ranks you against other candidates based on keyword matches
  • Filters out "low-match" candidates before human review
Bottom line: If your resume isn't optimized for ATS, it doesn't matter how qualified you are—hiring managers may never see your application.

The 5-Step ATS Optimization Process

1

Understand How ATS Works

Before optimizing, understand what you're optimizing for. ATS systems vary, but most follow this process:

  1. Parse: Extract text from your resume file
  2. Structure: Map information to fields (Name, Email, Experience, Education)
  3. Match: Compare your content against job requirements
  4. Score: Assign a match percentage (typically 0-100%)
  5. Filter: Pass only high-scoring candidates to recruiters

Common ATS platforms: Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, Taleo, iCIMS, BambooHR

2

Extract Keywords from Job Descriptions

Keywords are the foundation of ATS optimization. Here's how to identify them:

Types of Keywords to Extract:

Keyword Type Examples Where to Include
Hard Skills Python, SQL, Project Management, Excel Skills section, Experience bullets
Soft Skills Leadership, Communication, Problem-solving Summary, Experience bullets
Job Titles Software Engineer, Product Manager Work experience headers
Certifications PMP, AWS Certified, CPA Certifications section
Industry Terms Agile, SaaS, B2B, KPI Throughout resume
Tools/Software Salesforce, Jira, Tableau Skills section, Experience

Keyword Extraction Method:

  1. Collect 3-5 job descriptions for your target role
  2. Copy all text into a document
  3. Highlight repeated terms (appear in 2+ descriptions)
  4. Prioritize "required" over "preferred" qualifications
  5. Create a master list of 15-25 keywords
3

Use ATS-Friendly Formatting

Even perfectly keyword-optimized content fails if the ATS can't parse it. Follow these formatting rules:

✅ ATS-Friendly Formatting Checklist

Use standard fonts: Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, Georgia
Font size: 10-12pt for body, 14-16pt for headers
Margins: 0.5-1 inch on all sides
Single-column layout (avoid tables for main content)
Standard section headings: Experience, Education, Skills
Save as .docx (most compatible) or PDF (if requested)
Avoid headers/footers for important information
Use standard bullet points (•, -, *) not custom graphics
Left-align text (avoid justified alignment)
Include contact info in body, not header
❌ Avoid These ATS Killers:
  • Tables and multi-column layouts (ATS reads left-to-right, top-to-bottom)
  • Graphics, icons, or images containing text
  • Text boxes or shapes
  • Headers and footers (ATS may skip them)
  • Creative fonts or symbols
  • PDFs with scanned images (use text-based PDFs)
4

Optimize Content with Keywords

Now integrate your keywords naturally throughout your resume:

Where to Place Keywords:

  • Professional Summary (2-3 sentences): Include 3-5 top keywords naturally
  • Skills Section: List all relevant hard skills from your keyword research
  • Work Experience: Use keywords in bullet points with context
  • Job Titles: If accurate, mirror the title from the job description
  • Certifications: Include full certification names with acronyms

Keyword Integration Examples:

Bad: "Responsible for project management and data analysis."

Good: "Led project management for 12-person team using Agile methodology; performed data analysis in Python and SQL to identify $500K cost savings."

Pro Tip: Use both acronyms and full terms: "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" or "Customer Relationship Management (CRM)"—you don't know which the ATS is programmed to detect.
5

Test with an ATS Scanner

Before submitting, test your resume against the job description using an ATS checker:

What ATS Scanners Check:

  • Keyword match percentage
  • Missing hard and soft skills
  • Formatting issues
  • Section completeness
  • Contact information parsing
Target Score: Aim for 80%+ match rate. Most ATS pass candidates scoring 75% or higher to human review.

Common ATS Optimization Mistakes

Mistake Why It Hurts Fix
Keyword stuffing ATS flags as spam; humans reject Use keywords naturally with context
Creative formatting ATS can't parse tables, graphics Simple, single-column layout
PDF with images ATS sees blank document Use text-based PDF or .docx
Missing keywords Low match score = filtered out Extract and include all required skills
Generic resume Low relevance for specific role Tailor for each application

Key Takeaways

  1. ATS is a gatekeeper: 75% of resumes never reach human eyes. Optimization is essential.
  2. Keywords are critical: Extract them from job descriptions and use them naturally.
  3. Format for parsing: Simple, standard formatting beats creative design for ATS.
  4. Test before submitting: Use an ATS scanner to verify 80%+ match rate.
  5. Tailor every application: Generic resumes score poorly. Customize for each role.
